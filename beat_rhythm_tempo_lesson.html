History of Rock 'n' Roll — Unit 1: What Does It Sound Like?

The foundation of music

Before you can hear anything else in a piece of music — the melody, the harmony, the lyrics — you feel the pulse. That pulse is the beat, and everything else in a song is organized around it. Understanding how beat, rhythm, tempo, meter, and syncopation work gives you a framework for listening to any piece of music, in any genre, from any era.

These concepts are closely related, and they're easy to confuse. This lesson will help you hear the difference between them and identify each one in songs you already know.

Use these two tools to build your feel for beat and rhythm. Start with Tap the Beat to practice finding the pulse in real songs — then try the Step Sequencer to build your own rhythmic patterns and hear how meter and syncopation work in practice.
