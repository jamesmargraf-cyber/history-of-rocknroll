What is a Stereo Map?

When you listen to a recorded song, the sounds you hear aren't all coming from the same place. Engineers and producers deliberately place different instruments at different positions in the stereo field — the space between your left and right ears.

Stereo mapping is the practice of listening carefully and identifying where each element sits in that space. It trains your ears to hear a recording as a constructed sonic environment, not just a wall of sound.

Key concepts from this lesson

  • The stereo field runs from hard left to hard right, with center in between
  • Producers use panning to separate instruments and create space
  • Lead vocals and kick drum are almost always centered
  • Guitar, keys, and backing vocals are often spread left and right
  • Stereo width contributes to a recording's sense of size and depth
